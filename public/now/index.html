April 22 2026 — Bangkok Airport, Thailand

  • Transit: I’m sitting in Bangkok airport about to fly to Korea for a 12-hour layover, then onward to Seattle and Portland. Three weeks in Thailand went fast.
  • Bangkok: I didn’t even plan on coming here, but it ended up being night and day from winter Tokyo. The biggest difference was warmth—both the weather and the people. The city feels alive, energetic, and open.
  • Songkran: One of the most fun experiences I’ve had in years. Adults and kids laughing together, everyone joining in. Going with my friend Soren made it easier to jump in. I hadn’t had a proper water fight since childhood.
  • King Rama IX: Visiting the Rattanakosin Exhibition Hall was a highlight. For 100 baht, I got a two-hour guided tour and learned why so many Thai people deeply loved and respected him. Real leadership leaves a lasting emotional memory.
  • Movement: Bolt and Grab motorbike rides were unforgettable. Twenty minutes for just a few dollars, flying through traffic with the wind in your face. Half transportation, half roller coaster, half front-row documentary of city life.
  • City Design: Iconsiam and Terminal 21 showed how Bangkok understands public life. Iconsiam feels world-class with river views and open vertical space. Terminal 21’s Pier 21 food court might be the best value food experience anywhere.
  • People: Thai people were easy to talk to—friendly, smiling, warm. Many spoke some English too, more than I expected. It made daily life smoother and created more chances for connection.
  • Serendipity: Random choices often led to good moments—switching hotels, sitting by a rooftop pool, wandering malls, ferry rides, talking to strangers. Bangkok rewarded curiosity.
  • Friends: I’m grateful I already had friends from Bangkok through my Shanghai chapter. Reconnecting, reminiscing, and seeing where life brought everyone gave the trip extra meaning.
  • Healing: There’s an aura here. Heat, sunlight, massages, smiling people, street food, movement, abundance. Bangkok feels like a place where energy returns.
  • Thought: This is only a snippet of what I saw in three weeks. Some cities impress you with efficiency. Others remind you how to feel alive.
